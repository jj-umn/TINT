What is the Mayo Clinic CTSA?

The Mayo Clinic Center for Translational Science Activities (CTSA) is a multi-disciplinary research and education center that exists to provide training and mentoring, facilities and expert resources for research teams to support innovative clinical research and the process of turning research discoveries into real-world treatments. Education Resources

Training and mentoring the clinical and translational research teams of the future
  • Predoctoral, postdoctoral and research coordination programs
  • Mentored career development program
  • Continuing education and training at all levels

Research Resources

Providing staff and infrastructure to support innovative clinical and translational research

  • Inpatient, outpatient and moblie clinical research unit (CRU)
  • Translational technology core labs and services

Service Center

Streamlining the complex clinical research process with one-stop study team services

  • Consultation services (biostatistics, study design, research recruitment, minority inclusion)
  • Regulatory Affairs (FDA, IND/IDE)
  • Institutional liaisons
  • Intramural funding programs

Community Engagement

Connecting researchers, physicians and the community to apply research to real life

  • Office for Diversity in Clinical Research
  • Mayo Health System Practice-Based Research Network
  • Knowledge Translation Research Unit
  • Research Volunteer Resource
